About Me

I am a PhD researcher analyzing drug abuse vulnerability in psychosis via neurobiological mechanisms in a heritable rodent neonatal quinpirole model of dopamine dysregulation. My work focuses on modulation and stimulation of the mutually inhibitory adenosine A(2A)-metabotropic glutamate receptor type 5 (mGlu5)-dopamine D2 heteroreceptor complex.

We have completed fiber photometry live dopamine imaging in the nucleus accumbens to evaluate differences in reward response between the offspring of quinpirole-treated animals and controls. Additionally, we are evaluating susceptibility to drug relapse in our model via direct exposure to nicotine or stressful stimuli.

The primary focus of my dissertation is evaluating the role of α4β2 nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs) in a nicotine vapor conditioned place preference paradigm and its impact on neural plasticity markers in our heritable model of dopamine dysregulation. The overarching goal of this project is to elucidate novel mechanistic actions and treatment targets aimed at reducing behavioral deficits in psychosis while simultaneously reducing the rewarding effects of comorbid nicotine use.

Research Interests

  • Nicotine reward and relapse-related learning
  • Dopamine dysregulation and psychiatric risk
  • Transgenerational and developmental vulnerability mechanisms
  • Neural plasticity and receptor signaling in drug reinforcement
