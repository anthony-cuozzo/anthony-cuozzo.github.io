About Me

I am a PhD researcher studying comorbid nicotine use in models of psychosis, focusing on the role of dopamine dysregulation in reward-related learning and stress-induced nicotine seeking. My work integrates behavioral testing, molecular neurobiology, and systems-level analyses to identify mechanisms underlying vulnerability to substance use in psychiatric contexts.

Research Interests

  • Nicotine reward and relapse-related learning
  • Dopamine dysregulation and psychiatric risk
  • Transgenerational and developmental vulnerability mechanisms
  • Neural plasticity and receptor signaling in drug reinforcement
